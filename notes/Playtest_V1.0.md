# Playtest Feedback and Rule Updates (V1.1)

After the first playtest of the game, several issues were identified in terms of balance, resource distribution, and rule clarity. The following changes and observations summarize the main updates for the next version.

---

## City Attack and Pillage Mechanic

One major issue found during testing was that attacking a city had a cost that was too high compared with the potential benefits.

To address this problem, a new mechanic was added:

## Pillage City

When a player successfully enters a city, they can immediately take one resource from the city's resource slots.

The selected resource must come from an **undamaged resource slot**, and the player can choose any available slot.

After gaining the resource, the city receives damage.

After the damage is applied, the player checks whether there are still available resource slots that can be used. A city remains usable as long as at least one resource slot is still available, unless all slots have been damaged.

---

## Neutral City Special Rule

A special rule was added for attacking neutral cities.

If a player attacks a neutral city but their forces are defeated and forced to retreat, the noble will never be captured.

Instead, the noble immediately retreats.

This rule prevents unusual situations where a failed attack against a neutral city would create an unrealistic prisoner outcome.

---

# Food Resource Balance

Another major issue discovered during testing was related to the food resource.

Food is extremely important because it affects military development and political expansion. However, the current amount and distribution of food resources are insufficient.

Because food is:

- Unevenly distributed between regions
- Too limited in quantity

Some players are unable to recruit enough troops.

Another problem is that even if a player has large amounts of wealth and labor, they may still be unable to increase their city's political influence to higher levels, such as reaching K3.

Therefore, the amount and distribution of food resources need further adjustment.

---

# Map Topology and Balance

The current map topology may also require redesign.

Some city connections may create unfair strategic situations, making certain cities naturally stronger or weaker because of their geographical position.

The connection structure between cities will need further testing and possible revision.

---

# Victory Point Calculation

During testing, we discovered that the VP calculation system did not include military value.

Many resources are converted into armies during the game. If armies are not counted, the final score does not accurately represent a player's actual resources and achievements.

Therefore, future versions will include military units in VP calculation.

---

# Resource-to-VP Conversion

The conversion ratio between resources and Victory Points also requires further adjustment.

The current ratio may create an imbalance between the two major factions, especially for Tikal.

Without considering military value, Tikal may be placed at a disadvantage.

Further balancing tests are needed to determine a more appropriate conversion ratio.

---

# Rule Clarification

Several rules also need clearer explanations.

The main areas requiring clarification include:

- City occupation rules
- Noble recruitment rules
- The relationship between recruitment and political influence

The rulebook structure also needs revision.

Different rule sections should be separated more clearly so that players can find specific mechanics more easily during gameplay.
