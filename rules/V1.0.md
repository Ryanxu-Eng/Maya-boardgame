# Tikal–Kaan v1.0

**Two-Player Strategy Board Game Rulebook**

---

## I. Game Overview

*Tikal–Kaan* is a two-player strategy game set against the backdrop of political competition among Classic Maya city-states. The two players control the two major powers, **Tikal** and **Kaan / Calakmul**, and compete for regional dominance through political influence, sacrifice, resource management, military expansion, and city occupation. 

Cities in the game simultaneously have two different states:

- **Political Alignment** represents which side the local ruling elite and society support in the long term;
- **Military Control** represents which side’s army currently occupies the city.

Therefore, it is entirely possible for a city to be:

> Politically at Tikal Level 3,
> 
> 
> but militarily occupied by Kaan.
> 

The game’s core strategy revolves around three approaches:

- **Political Expansion:** Slower and resource-consuming, but provides stable resources, local recruitment capabilities, and combat support;
- **Military Expansion:** Can rapidly change the situation on the map and temporarily exploit enemy cities, but requires garrisons to maintain control and may cause city Damage;
- **Noble System:** Connects warfare, military command, prisoner ransom, and Grand Sacrifice.

The game lasts **7 rounds**. In each round, the **Kaan player always completes their entire turn first**, followed by the **Tikal player completing their entire turn**.

After Round 7 ends, VP is calculated. The player with the highest VP wins.

---

## II. Initial Setup

One player controls **Kaan / Calakmul**, and the other controls **Tikal**.

The Kaan player always acts first in each round.

Each player begins with:

### Starting Resources

- 2 Food
- 2 Wealth
- 1 Labor

### Starting Military Units

- 3 regular Warriors
- 1 Noble

All starting units are placed in the player’s own capital:

- **Tikal player:** Tikal
- **Kaan player:** Calakmul

Players still collect resources normally during the first round. The first Resource Phase is not skipped.

### Military Unit Limit

Each player may have a maximum of:

**14 military units**

belonging to them at the same time.

Both regular Warriors and Nobles count toward this 14-unit limit.

For example:

- 12 Warriors + 2 Nobles = 14
- 11 Warriors + 3 Nobles = 14

Each player may have a maximum of:

**3 Nobles**

at the same time.

A Noble captured by the enemy still counts toward the original player’s:

- Noble limit;
- 14-unit total military unit limit.

Dead regular Warriors return to the supply and may be recruited again.

Sacrificed Nobles likewise return to the original player’s supply and may be recruited again later.

---

## III. Resource Types and Uses

There are three types of resources in the game.

### Food

Primarily used for:

- Recruiting regular Warriors;
- Paying army upkeep;
- Sacrifice.

### Wealth

Represents jade, cacao, feathers, various valuables, and elite exchange goods.

Primarily used for:

- Sacrifice;
- Recruiting Nobles.

### Labor

Represents population, labor power, and the capacity for social mobilization.

Primarily used for:

- Recruiting regular Warriors;
- Recruiting Nobles;
- Repairing city Damage.

### Major Resource Costs

| Action | Cost |
| --- | --- |
| Recruit 1 Warrior | 1 Food + 1 Labor |
| Recruit 1 Noble | 2 Wealth + 1 Labor |
| Repair 1 Damage | 2 Labor |
| Neutral ↔︎ Level 1 | 1 Food + 1 Wealth |
| Level 1 ↔︎ Level 2 | 1 Food + 2 Wealth |
| Level 2 ↔︎ Level 3 | 1 Food + 1 Labor + 2 Wealth |
| Noble Ransom | Any combination of 3 resources |

There is no limit to the number of resources that may be stored.

At the end of the game, all remaining resources can also be converted into VP.

## IV. Cities

There are **7 major cities** in the game.

| City | Initial Political Status | Base Resource Production |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tikal | Tikal Capital | 1 Food + 1 Wealth + 2 Labor |
| Calakmul | Kaan Capital | 1 Food + 1 Wealth + 2 Labor |
| Palenque | Tikal Level 1 | 2 Wealth + 1 Labor |
| Waka’ | Kaan Level 1 | 2 Wealth + 1 Food |
| Dos Pilas | Kaan Level 1 | 2 Labor + 1 Food |
| Naranjo | Neutral | 2 Food + 1 Wealth |
| Caracol | Neutral | 2 Food + 1 Labor |

Regular cities have **3 Resource Slots**.

The two capitals have **4 Resource Slots**.

When a player can use only some of a city’s Resource Slots, that player may choose which undamaged resources to collect.

### Direct Connections Between Cities

The current connections between cities on the map are:

- Palenque — Calakmul
- Palenque — Waka’
- Calakmul — Waka’
- Calakmul — Naranjo
- Waka’ — Tikal
- Waka’ — Dos Pilas
- Tikal — Dos Pilas
- Tikal — Naranjo
- Tikal — Caracol
- Naranjo — Caracol
- Dos Pilas — Caracol

**There is no direct connection between Tikal and Calakmul.**

These direct connections determine not only some military movement, but also the political network connectivity required for Sacrifice.

### Political Alignment

Each non-capital city has a 7-space political track:

`Kaan 3 — Kaan 2 — Kaan 1 — Neutral — Tikal 1 — Tikal 2 — Tikal 3`

A city’s Political Alignment does not automatically change because of military occupation.

Normally, when a city reaches different political levels for a player:

| Political Level | Usable Resource Slots | Local Recruitment Allowed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Level 1 | 1 | No |
| Level 2 | 2 | Yes |
| Level 3 | 3 | Yes |

Political Support also affects city battles:

| Political Level | Combat Bonus for the Corresponding Player |
| --- | --- |
| Neutral | 0 |
| Level 1 | 0 |
| Level 2 | 1 |
| Level 3 | 2 |

Political Support belongs to the side supported by the political track, not to the current military occupier.

For example:

> A Tikal Level 3 city is occupied by Kaan.
> 
> 
> When the Kaan garrison defends, it can use the city’s defenses, but if Tikal launches a counterattack, Tikal receives:
> 
> **+2 local Political Support.**
> 

### Military Occupation and Resource Use

A city is considered under military occupation only when enemy troops are physically stationed there.

If all occupying troops leave:

- Military occupation immediately ends.
- The political track does not change as a result.

Resource use when occupying cities with different political statuses is as follows:

#### Occupying an Enemy-Aligned City

May use at most:

**1 undamaged Resource Slot**

Local recruitment is not allowed.

#### Occupying a Neutral City

May use at most:

**2 undamaged Resource Slots**

Local recruitment is not allowed.

#### After the City’s Politics Have Advanced to Your Level 1 While Occupied

As long as your troops remain stationed there, you may use:

**2 undamaged Resource Slots**

but still may not recruit locally.

If the garrison leaves, the city returns to the normal Level 1 effect:

**Only 1 Resource Slot may be used.**

#### Reaching Your Level 2

Normal political rules are restored:

**2 Resource Slots**, and local recruitment is allowed.

#### Reaching Your Level 3

You may use:

**All 3 Resource Slots**, and local recruitment is allowed.

Thus, military occupation can temporarily improve resource access, but it cannot replace genuine political integration.

### City Defense and Damage

Regular cities have:

- 3 Resource Slots;
- 2 City Defense Slots.

The two capitals have:

- 4 Resource Slots;
- 4 City Defense Slots.

Each undamaged City Defense Slot provides:

**+1 Combat Strength**

in a city battle.

The Damage limit for every city is:

**3 Damage**

Each time a city is successfully captured by the enemy:

**It gains 1 Damage.**

Field battles do not cause Damage.

Failed sieges do not cause Damage.

Each Damage disables:

- 1 Resource Slot; or
- 1 City Defense Slot.

The player who captured the city and becomes its current military controller chooses which currently undamaged slot is disabled.

Damage does not change Political Alignment.

Pay:

**2 Labor**

to repair 1 Damage and reactivate one damaged slot.

### Special Rules for Capitals

Tikal and Calakmul:

- Have no political track;
- Always belong to their original player;
- Have 4 points of base City Defense;
- Have 4 Resource Slots.

While an enemy occupies a capital:

- The original player cannot collect resources from the capital;
- The original player cannot recruit units there;
- The occupier may use at most 1 undamaged Resource Slot;
- The occupier cannot recruit there.

As soon as all occupying troops leave:

**The capital immediately returns to the control of its original player.**

Each time a player successfully captures the enemy capital, the attacker chooses one resource type:

- Food;
- Wealth;
- Labor.

The attacker then immediately takes:

**All resources of that type from the opponent’s stockpile.**

If the capital is later retaken and then falls again, this effect may be triggered again.

## V. Empty Military Areas

Some of the lines connecting cities on the map enclose officially designated empty military areas.

These areas are military spaces. Armies may:

- Remain stationed there;
- Move into them;
- Retreat from them;
- Fight field battles there.

However, empty areas:

- Have no resources;
- Have no political track;
- Have no City Defense;
- Have no Damage;
- Cannot recruit units;
- Cannot conduct Sacrifice.

Most importantly:

**Empty areas can never provide political connectivity for Sacrifice.**

Even if:

- There are no enemy troops in the empty area;
- Your own troops are stationed in the empty area;

it still cannot be treated as a valid political or sacrificial supply route.

Empty areas serve only military maneuver and field battle functions.

Wilderness outside the map that is not explicitly designated as a military area is not an enterable space.

## VI. Turn Sequence

In each round, the Kaan player first completes all phases, followed by the Tikal player completing all phases.

A player’s complete turn proceeds in the following order:

1. Resource Phase
2. Political Phase
3. Military Phase
4. Upkeep Phase
5. Recruitment & Reconstruction Phase

The two players do not alternate between individual phases.

### 1. Resource Phase: Collect Resources

The player first collects resources from all cities they are currently able to use.

The amount of resources collected is jointly determined by:

- The city’s political level;
- Its current military occupation status;
- The city’s Damage.

Resources are still collected normally in the first round.

### 2. Political Phase: Politics and Sacrifice

During each Political Phase, the player performs:

**Political actions (multiple times)**

The player may choose:

- Regular Sacrifice;
- Noble Grand Sacrifice.

The two cannot both be performed in the same city during the same turn.

#### Regular Sacrifice

Choose an eligible city and move its political track:

**1 space toward your side.**

The cost is determined by the political level crossed.

Between Neutral and Level 1:

**1 Food + 1 Wealth**

Between Level 1 and Level 2:

**1 Food + 2 Wealth**

Between Level 2 and Level 3:

**1 Food + 1 Labor + 2 Wealth**

When advancing from the enemy political side toward Neutral, pay according to the current level in the same way:

- Enemy 3 → Enemy 2: 4 resources
- Enemy 2 → Enemy 1: 3 resources
- Enemy 1 → Neutral: 2 resources

#### Sacrifice Connectivity Requirement

The target city must be directly connected to at least one:

**Friendly city belonging to you**

Friendly cities belonging to you include:

- Your own capital, provided it is not occupied by enemy troops;
- Your own Level 1, Level 2, or Level 3 cities.

In addition, that friendly city must not currently be under enemy military occupation.

The following cannot provide Sacrifice connectivity:

- Neutral cities;
- Enemy-aligned cities;
- Your own cities currently occupied by enemy troops;
- Empty military areas;
- Empty military areas occupied by your own troops.

If the target city has no directly connected eligible friendly city, that city is:

**Isolated**

An Isolated city cannot perform:

- Regular Sacrifice;
- Noble Grand Sacrifice.

However, its existing Political Alignment does not automatically decrease.

If a city is currently under enemy military occupation:

**The non-occupying player cannot perform Sacrifice in that city.**

#### Noble Grand Sacrifice

A player may sacrifice an enemy Noble prisoner currently in their possession.

The target city must:

1. Be militarily occupied by the player’s own troops;
2. Be a city that was previously captured through military action and has remained continuously occupied;
3. Still have its political track on the enemy side;
4. Not be Isolated.

A Grand Sacrifice moves the political track:

**2 consecutive spaces toward your side.**

First calculate all Sacrifice resources that would normally be required to move those two spaces. Then:

**The sacrificed Noble may substitute for any 2 resource points among those costs.**

For example:

`Enemy Level 1 → Neutral → Your Level 1`

Normally, this requires 4 resource points in total.

After using Grand Sacrifice:

**Spend the Noble + pay 2 additional points of the required resources.**

The sacrificed Noble returns to the original owner’s supply and may be recruited again later.

### 3. Military Phase: Movement and Combat

Each military unit has:

**1 Movement Point per turn**

A unit may move at most once per turn.

Possible movement includes:

- City → directly adjacent city;
- City → adjacent empty military area;
- Empty military area → adjacent city;
- Movement between empty areas explicitly shown as adjacent on the map.

Armies may split up when moving.

When entering a space containing enemy troops:

**Combat begins immediately.**

When entering a city with no player troops but whose Political Alignment is Neutral or aligned with the enemy:

**A city battle also occurs.**

The city itself participates in the defense.

#### Basic Noble Rules

Nobles count toward the 14-unit total military unit limit.

Within the same military space, each player may have at most:

**1 Noble**

One regular Warrior provides:

**1 point of Base Combat Strength**

A Noble fighting alone provides:

**1 point of Base Combat Strength**

If a Noble participates in combat together with at least 1 regular Warrior:

**The Noble provides 2 points of Base Combat Strength.**

Therefore:

- 3 Warriors + 1 Noble = 5 Base Combat Strength;
- 1 Warrior + 1 Noble = 3 Base Combat Strength;
- 1 Noble alone = 1 Base Combat Strength.

A Noble may remain alone in a city and, in theory, may also move or attack alone, but receives no additional leadership bonus.

#### Noble Special Ability

Each Noble newly entering the game enters in the **Ready** state.

A Noble has a special command ability that may be used once and only in:

**City battles**

Before either side rolls the dice:

1. The attacker first declares whether to use the Noble ability;
2. The defender then declares whether to use it.

If only one side uses the ability:

- Both sides normally roll 1D6;
- The side using the ability takes the higher result of the two dice as its own final die result;
- The other side still uses its own original die result.

If both sides use the ability:

- The two abilities cancel each other out;
- Each side uses its own original die result;
- However, both Nobles’ abilities are considered spent.

After using the ability, flip the Noble to indicate that it is **Exhausted**.

The ability:

- Does not refresh each round;
- Does not refresh after capture;
- Does not refresh after ransom.

Only when a Noble leaves the game and is later recruited back onto the map does it return in the **Ready** state.

#### Combat Strength

#### Field Battles

Fixed Combat Strength includes:

- Warriors;
- Nobles and their leadership bonus.

#### City Battles

Also include:

- Effective City Defense;
- Political Support.

The side that currently has military control of the city may use its remaining City Defense.

Political Support always belongs to the side indicated by the political track.

#### Dice and Victory

Both sides roll:

**1D6**

Normally:

`Total Combat Strength = Fixed Combat Strength + Final Effective Die Result`

#### Field Battles

The side with the higher Total Combat Strength wins.

On a tie:

**The defender wins.**

#### City Battles

The side with the higher Total Combat Strength wins.

On a tie:

**The attacker wins.**

Once the winner has been determined, the result is not recalculated because of casualties suffered afterward.

#### Combat Casualties

Both sides’ final effective die results also determine additional casualties.

If the opponent’s final die result is:

| Opponent’s Final Die Result | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| 1–4 | No additional casualties |
| 5–6 | Inflict 1 regular Warrior casualty on the enemy |

The losing side then automatically:

**Loses 1 additional regular Warrior.**

If the final difference in Total Combat Strength is:

**4 or more**

the losing side:

**Loses 1 additional regular Warrior.**

Normally:

- The winner loses 0–1 Warrior;
- The loser loses 1–3 Warriors.

A Noble is never directly removed as a normal combat casualty.

#### Ungarrisoned Cities

A city defends itself even if no player’s troops are stationed there.

For example, a fully undamaged Neutral regular city has:

`2 City Defense + 1D6`

If it is an enemy Level 3 city:

`2 City Defense + 2 Political Support + 1D6`

Therefore, attacking a city with no player garrison can still result in casualties.

#### Noble Capture

If, after the losing side resolves all normal casualties:

**There are no regular Warriors remaining alongside the Noble,**

that Noble is immediately captured by the winner.

Therefore, a Noble fighting alone:

**Is immediately captured if defeated.**

If the losing side still has Warriors but has no legal retreat space:

1. Lose 1 additional regular Warrior;
2. The accompanying Noble is captured.
3. The remaining troops may then retreat to any friendly city whose Political Alignment favors their side, or to their own capital.

#### Retreat

A defeated army must retreat to one adjacent legal space.

For an empty military area, the only requirement is:

**No enemy troops are stationed there.**

A city must satisfy both of the following:

- No enemy troops are present;
- It is at least your Level 1, or it is your own capital.

Therefore:

- Neutral cities cannot be used as retreat destinations;
- Enemy-aligned cities cannot be used as retreat destinations;
- A city that is merely under your military occupation while still politically aligned with the enemy cannot be used as a legal city retreat destination.

#### Ransoming Noble Prisoners

The ransom for a Noble is fixed at:

**3 resources**

The holder of the prisoner actively offers the ransom.

Once offered:

**The offer cannot be withdrawn.**

The Noble’s original owner may choose to accept or reject.

If accepted:

The paying player chooses which 3 resources to pay.

For example:

- 3 Food;
- 3 Wealth;
- 2 Labor + 1 Food;
- 1 Food + 1 Wealth + 1 Labor.

Any combination is valid as long as the total is 3.

The holder of the prisoner must accept that combination.

If the original owner refuses:

The Noble remains captured.

### 4. Upkeep Phase: Army Upkeep

After the Military Phase ends, the player pays Food based on the number of:

**Regular Warriors currently belonging to that player**

Nobles do not require upkeep, but still count toward the 14-unit total unit limit.

Upkeep costs are as follows:

| Number of Regular Warriors | Food |
| --- | --- |
| 0–1 | 0 |
| 2–4 | 1 |
| 5–7 | 2 |
| 8–10 | 3 |
| 11–13 | 4 |
| 14 | 5 |

For example:

- 6 Warriors = 2 Food
- 7 Warriors = 2 Food
- 8 Warriors = 3 Food
- 9 Warriors = 3 Food

If a player cannot pay the required Food:

**They must remove regular Warriors until the remaining army falls into an upkeep bracket they can afford.**

### 5. Recruitment & Reconstruction Phase: Recruitment and Reconstruction

After paying army upkeep, the player may use remaining resources to recruit new units or repair cities.

#### Recruiting Warriors

Each costs:

**1 Food + 1 Labor**

They may be recruited in:

- Your own capital;
- Your own Level 2 or Level 3 city that is not occupied by enemy troops.

As long as the player has enough resources and does not exceed the 14-unit total limit, multiple Warriors may be recruited in one turn.

#### Recruiting Nobles

Each costs:

**2 Wealth + 1 Labor**

They may be recruited in:

- Your own capital;
- Your own Level 2 or Level 3 city that is not occupied by enemy troops.

Each player may recruit at most:

**1 Noble per turn.**

At the same time, the player may not exceed:

- The 3-Noble limit;
- The 14-unit total military unit limit.

A newly recruited Noble enters in the **Ready** state.

#### Repairing Cities

Pay:

**2 Labor**

to remove 1 Damage and reactivate one disabled Resource Slot or City Defense Slot.

As long as the player has enough resources, multiple Damage may be repaired during one phase.

Because the Military Phase has already ended, newly recruited military units:

**Cannot move this turn.**

## VII. VP Scoring and Game End

The game lasts:

**7 rounds**

Each round:

1. Kaan completes one full turn;
2. Tikal completes one full turn.

Immediately after Tikal’s turn in Round 7 ends, final scoring begins.

Players do not gain immediate VP directly for:

- Killing Warriors;
- Winning ordinary battles;
- Performing Sacrifice;
- Capturing ordinary cities.

These actions are all means of shaping the final political situation.

### Political Alignment VP

The five non-capital cities score according to their final position on the political track:

| Political Status | VP Awarded to the Corresponding Player |
| --- | --- |
| Level 1 | 1 VP |
| Level 2 | 3 VP |
| Level 3 | 5 VP |
| Neutral | 0 VP |

This is the main source of VP in the game.

### City Prosperity VP

Count:

- Your own capital, provided it is not occupied by the enemy at the end of the game;
- Your own Level 2 or Level 3 regular cities, provided they are not occupied by the enemy at the end of the game.

Score according to final Damage:

| Damage | VP |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | 2 VP |
| 1 | 1 VP |
| 2–3 | 0 VP |

### Final Military Occupation VP

If, at the end of the game, you are still militarily occupying a regular city that does not politically belong to you:

**1 VP**

If, at the end of the game, you are still militarily occupying the enemy capital:

**5 VP**

### Noble Prisoner VP

For each enemy Noble you still hold at the end of the game that has not been:

- Ransomed;
- Sacrificed;

gain:

**1 VP**

### Remaining Resource VP

Add together all of your remaining:

- Food;
- Wealth;
- Labor.

For every:

**3 resources, gain 1 VP**

rounded down.

For example:

- 5 resources = 1 VP
- 6 resources = 2 VP
- 8 resources = 2 VP
- 9 resources = 3 VP

### Final Score

Final VP is:

**Political Alignment VP**

＋ **City Prosperity VP**

＋ **Final Military Occupation VP**

＋ **Noble Prisoner VP**

＋ **Remaining Resource VP**

The player with the highest VP wins.

If both players have exactly the same VP, the first-edition test rules treat the result as a draw.
